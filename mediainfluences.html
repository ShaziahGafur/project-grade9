Islamic Portrayals in Media

My name is Khan is a Bollywood film directed and produced by Karan Johar, which serves as an excellent example of influencing viewers through media to fight against discrimination. Karan Johar creates a storyline of a man, Rizwan Khan, who journeys to express his message to America, “My name is Khan and I am not a terrorist.” This movie teaches viewers the effects of the 9/11 attacks to the religion of Islam. It teaches viewers how it is unacceptable to discriminate everyone of this specific category.

In ABC’s Lost television series, Sayid Jarrah, presented as the only Muslim character of the show, served for the Iraq’s Special Republican Guard and later served as an aggressive interrogator. Later in the show, Jarrah is perceived to be part of the anti-terrorism squad, however his true actions still show that he is a very violent man throughout all six seasons of the show. CBC’s Little Mosque on the Prairie was built to attempt to stop some discrimination against Muslims and remove these negative stereotypical interpretations.

Why not blame the media?

Terrorist attacks from around the world have altered the perception people have of Muslims, especially since the attacks of 9/11. However, the true misunderstanding behind these stereotypes is from false media portrayals of Islam. Media influences have increased negative misconceptions on this religion, including multiple harmful stereotypes. Numerous TV shows and movies influence people to either support this type of discrimination, or fight against it. Many of the stereotypes created have been due to false assumptions based on media portrayals. Common Islamic stereotypes include:

  • “Muslims are Terrorists”
  • “She is forced to wear the hijab”
  • “All Arabs are Muslim”
  • “Islam is intolerable to other religions”
  • “Muslims live in the Medieval Times and are unable to adapt to the current world”
  • “Muslims are all foreigners who cannot adapt to Western societies”

Islamic Discrimination Developing

Every race in the world has been inflicted with some sort of inequality and discrimination. In our 20th century, racism has been transitioning into discrimination against multi cultures and colours. Since the tragic 9/11 attacks, racism has been linked with religion, as Muslims have become the biggest target.

Media experts, producers and advertisers use racial and religious discrimination to create interest and more ideas through business. However, it is still unacceptable to discriminate Muslims through media, as this will only create more discrimination. It is not fair to represent a huge portion of the world population through false negative assumptions. In conclusion, Muslims should not be represented by popular movies and other forms of entertainment that portray this religion in a negative way.
